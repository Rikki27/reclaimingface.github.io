About this project

See the headings below for an in-depth introduction to Reclaiming Face

Scope

In its first year this project will focus on Canada, United States, Mexico and Peru. During an expanded second year, data will be added for subsequent South and Central American countries. The initial four countries have been chosen because of their specific links to Chinese immigration.

Functionality

Reclaiming Face will feature an interactive heat map as a browsing interface, as well as a keyword search functionality. To browse through time, users slide the timeline to alter the heatmap in accordance with population in particular locations through North and South America. To browse through place, users can click and drag the map. The heat map will also include time-dependent map points that correlate to particular towns and rural areas.

Audience

The target audience for Reclaiming Face includes anyone who is interested in learning more about the history of Chinese immigration. The point of this website is to raise awareness for a largely unknwown historical narrative, so we want this website to be accessible for people without any historical or academic background, as well as scholars.

Data, Info, Content

Reclaiming Face will provide free, online access to selected materials from a number of diverse primary source collections focusing on the diasporic Chinese immigration experience in the Americas. The core content of the site is the statistical data visualised on a heatmap; original historic work accessed through the map and accompanying primary, digitised content embedded in the thematic pages. This will include digitised, currently available photos, documents, moving image and recorded sound artefacts. Data will also be drawn from partnered researchers and community-based organizations

Clicking on map points will bring up a box with a small introductory text statement (original) and particular photo or media that represents a more granulated perspective on immigration (from another website). In this box the user will have the opportunity to click further into a page of original research on a variety of themes. Each page will include embedded digitised, primary content drawn from other repositories (e.g. digital public library of america) and links to supplementary material (e.g. education curriculum, further research, other websites). Searching will be based around person, place, ship or digital collection and results will take users to the relevant place on the map. Open source tools will be used to create this project (e.g. OpenLayers, Timeliner). It will be publicly available and open access from a free standing

Outcomes

This project will result in a website based around an interactive map and timeline. The website will contain new research (statistical population data, thematic narrative pages), and serve as a portal to other digital archives and collections (video, photos, artefacts) held by relevant online repositories
